SOCCER 2026: LIVE SCORES — Review notes

TIMING: This app is a companion for the 2026 international soccer tournament (June 11 – July 19, 2026). Live scores and live standings only exist while matches are being played; between matches the same screens show the full schedule and latest results. That is expected behavior, not a bug.

1. SCREEN RECORDING: Attached, captured on a physical iPhone running the latest iOS. Flow: launch -> Schedule (all matches, local kickoff times) -> open a match -> Follow (triggers the app's only permission prompt: local notifications) -> Groups -> Bracket -> Teams -> a team page with squad -> Favorites. There are no account, login, payment, subscription, user-generated-content, or App Tracking Transparency flows.

2. TESTED ON: [EDIT: e.g. iPhone 15 Pro (iOS 26.x), iPhone 13 (iOS 26.x)]; also iPhone 17 Pro Max simulator. Minimum iOS 17.0. iPhone only, portrait.

3. PURPOSE: A free, ad-free way for soccer fans to follow the tournament — all 104 matches in the user's local time zone, live scores, group standings, the knockout bracket, and kickoff reminders 15 minutes before matches they follow. No ads, no in-app purchases, no accounts.

4. SETUP / CREDENTIALS: None. No login or demo account exists. The app is fully usable on first launch; the notification prompt can be declined and everything else still works.

5. EXTERNAL SERVICES: Live scores and standings come from API-Football (api-football.com, paid data subscription) through our own Cloudflare Worker proxy; requests contain no personal data. The schedule, teams, and bracket are also bundled in the app (OpenFootball, public-domain data) so it works offline. No analytics, advertising, tracking, crash-reporting, authentication, or payment SDKs.

6. REGIONS: Behaves identically in every region and storefront; free everywhere. English UI; dates and times follow the device locale.

7. THIRD-PARTY MATERIAL: Independent, unofficial app — not affiliated with, sponsored by, or endorsed by FIFA or any federation, and it does not claim to be. No official emblem, mascot, or trophy imagery; the icon is a generic soccer ball; teams are shown as standard iOS flag emoji (no crest images are shipped or displayed). Match dates, venues, and team names are factual information; "World Cup" appears only descriptively in listing text. We will gladly adjust anything the review team flags: john@johnvdoan.com.
