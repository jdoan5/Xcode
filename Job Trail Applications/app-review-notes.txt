OVERVIEW
Job Trail is a simple, fully offline job-application tracker. The user records jobs they have applied to — company, role, location, date applied, posting URL, and notes — and tracks each application's status (Applied, Interviewing, Offer, Rejected, Withdrawn). All data is stored locally on the device with Apple's SwiftData. There is no account, no server, no network use, no third-party SDK, no advertising, no analytics, and no tracking, and the app requests no special permissions.

3. PURPOSE, AUDIENCE, PROBLEM, VALUE
Purpose: a clean, private way for job seekers to keep their entire job search organized in one place. Problem it solves: job applications are usually scattered across emails, spreadsheets, and job boards, so it is easy to lose track of where things stand; Job Trail puts every application, its status, the posting link, and your notes into one fast, private list. Value: see your whole pipeline at a glance, filter by status, search by company or role, and never lose track of an application. Target audience: anyone applying for jobs. The app is free, with no ads, no in-app purchases, no subscriptions, and no accounts.

4. SETUP, ACCESS, CREDENTIALS, SAMPLE FILES
No login, account, email, demo credentials, or sample files are required, and nothing needs to be configured. The app is fully usable the moment it launches. On first launch the list is empty with an "Add a job" button; tap + (top right) to add an application, tap any job to view or edit it and change its status, and swipe a row left to delete it.

5. EXTERNAL SERVICES, TOOLS, OR PLATFORMS
None. The app makes no network requests and integrates no data providers, authentication services, payment processors, AI services, analytics, advertising, or crash-reporting SDKs. The only data is what the user types, stored on the device with SwiftData. Tapping a saved job's link opens that web page in the user's own browser; the app itself transmits nothing.

6. REGIONAL DIFFERENCES
None. The app behaves identically in every region and storefront — free everywhere, with no region-locked content, features, or pricing. The interface is in English; dates follow the device's locale.
