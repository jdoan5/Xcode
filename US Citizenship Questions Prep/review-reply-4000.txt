US CITIZENSHIP CIVICS TEST 128 — Reply to Guideline 2.1, items in order.

OVERVIEW: A fully offline study app for the 2025 U.S. naturalization civics test, containing the official 128 USCIS questions and answers (M-1778, 09/25). Modes: a practice test that mirrors the real interview (20 questions, 12 to pass, ends early once decided), fill-in-the-blank typing practice, and a searchable study list. Eight answers change with elections (President, Vice President, Speaker, Chief Justice, senators, representative, governor, state capital); the user saves the current names in "My Officials," and the app grades against them and links to the official USCIS test-updates page. All data stays on the device (UserDefaults). No account, no server, no network use, no third-party SDKs, no ads, no analytics, no tracking, no permissions requested.

1. SCREEN RECORDING: Attached, captured on a physical iPhone running the latest iOS. It begins by launching the app, then: home screen (modes, 65/20 toggle, election notice) -> Practice Test, answering two questions (a correct pick turns green; a wrong pick turns red and reveals the answer) -> Fill in the Blank, typing "republic" and tapping Check -> Study, searching the list and opening a "May change" question with the election warning and uscis.gov link -> My Officials, typing a name. The app has NONE of the listed flows: no registration, login, or account deletion; no paid content or subscriptions; no user-generated content visible to others; no prompts for sensitive data or device capabilities (no ATT).

2. TESTED ON: Physical iPhone 17 Pro Max (iOS 26.5); also the iPhone 17 Pro Max simulator (iOS 26.5). Minimum iOS 17.0; iPhone only, portrait.

3. PURPOSE/AUDIENCE: Helps permanent residents prepare for the civics portion of the naturalization interview. The official list is a 19-page PDF, and several answers go stale after every election; this app turns it into realistic practice (early-stop rule, 65/20 senior option, recall practice rather than recognition) and handles the changing answers explicitly. Audience: green-card holders preparing to naturalize, citizenship-class teachers, and civics learners. Free; no ads, purchases, or accounts.

4. SETUP/ACCESS: Nothing to set up — no login, credentials, or sample files. Fully usable on first launch. Optionally, typing current officials' names in My Officials enables the eight election-dependent questions; left empty, those are skipped in practice modes (still visible in Study).

5. EXTERNAL SERVICES: None. The app makes no network requests; the whole question bank is compiled in. No data providers, authentication, payments, AI, analytics, advertising, or crash reporting. Links to uscis.gov, senate.gov, and house.gov open in the user's own browser; the app itself transmits nothing.

6. REGIONAL DIFFERENCES: None — identical, free, and unrestricted in every region and storefront. English interface (the language of the standard test).

7. REGULATED INDUSTRY / THIRD-PARTY MATERIAL: The content is from USCIS publication M-1778, a U.S. government work in the public domain (17 U.S.C. § 105). The app is an independent study aid, links to uscis.gov for authoritative updates, and its App Store description states it is not affiliated with or endorsed by USCIS or the U.S. government. No government seals, logos, or insignia are used (the icon is an original star design). Study content only; no legal or immigration advice.
