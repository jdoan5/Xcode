OVERVIEW
Budget Coach is a simple, fully offline monthly-budgeting app. The user enters their monthly income and what they spend across fixed categories (Housing, Food & Groceries, Transport, Shopping, Entertainment, Other) plus a Savings amount. The app calculates whether they are under or over budget and shows a 50/30/20 (Needs/Wants/Savings) breakdown. The user can tap "Coach me" to receive a few short, general budgeting tips. All data is stored locally on the device (the working budget in UserDefaults; saved monthly snapshots with Apple's SwiftData). There is no account, no server, no network use, no third-party SDK, no advertising, no analytics, and no tracking, and the app requests no special permissions.

3. PURPOSE, AUDIENCE, PROBLEM, VALUE
Purpose: a calm, private way for everyday people to see whether their monthly spending fits their income and to build a savings habit. Problem it solves: most people don't have a quick, private picture of where their money goes each month or how it compares to a sensible guideline. Budget Coach turns a few numbers into an instant under/over-budget result, a 50/30/20 breakdown, and a few plain-language tips. Value: understand your month at a glance, see your needs/wants/savings split, and track your savings rate over time. Target audience: anyone who wants help budgeting. The app is free, with no ads, no in-app purchases, no subscriptions, and no accounts.

4. SETUP, ACCESS, CREDENTIALS, SAMPLE FILES
No login, account, email, demo credentials, or sample files are required, and nothing needs to be configured. The app is fully usable the moment it launches. On first launch a short educational disclaimer is shown (tap "I understand"); then the Budget tab opens ready for input.

5. EXTERNAL SERVICES, TOOLS, OR PLATFORMS
None. The app makes no network requests and integrates no data providers, authentication services, payment processors, analytics, advertising, or crash-reporting SDKs. The coaching tips are generated entirely ON DEVICE using Apple's built-in Foundation Models framework (the same on-device model behind Apple Intelligence); no prompt or budget data is sent off the device. On devices where Apple Intelligence is unavailable, the app falls back to simple rule-based tips computed locally. The only data is what the user types, stored on the device.

6. REGIONAL DIFFERENCES
None. The app behaves identically in every region and storefront — free everywhere, with no region-locked content, features, or pricing. The user can choose their currency symbol in Settings. The interface is in English.

7. FINANCIAL ADVICE / REGULATED INDUSTRY
Budget Coach is a general-purpose budgeting and educational tool. It is NOT a financial institution, does not connect to any bank or account, does not move money, and does not provide personalized financial, investment, tax, or legal advice. The tips are general educational suggestions based only on the numbers the user typed and the well-known 50/30/20 guideline; they never recommend specific securities, funds, lenders, or products. An educational disclaimer to this effect is shown on first launch and is also visible on the Coach tab and in Settings. Any figures shown in the App Store screenshots are fictitious examples created only for the screenshots.
