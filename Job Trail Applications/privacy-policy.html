Privacy Policy

Job Trail

Job Trail is a simple tracker for your own job applications. It is built to collect nothing about you — no account, no tracking, no ads, no analytics, and no network connection at all.

Summary in one line: Everything you enter — your job applications and their details — is stored only on your iPhone. The app has no accounts, contacts no servers, and sends nothing anywhere.

What we collect

Nothing about you personally. There are no accounts to create, no email to enter, and we operate no server that could receive your data. The app uses no analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs.

What stays on your device

Everything you add to Job Trail is stored locally on your iPhone (using Apple's on-device SwiftData) and never leaves it:

  • The jobs you add — company, role, location, date applied, posting URL, and notes
  • The status you set for each application (Applied, Interviewing, Offer, Rejected, Withdrawn)

Deleting the app removes all of it. There is no cloud copy and no backup held by us.

Network use

Job Trail makes no network requests. It works entirely offline. The one exception is at your own initiative: if you tap a job's saved link, your iPhone opens that web address in your default browser. At that point your browser — not Job Trail — handles the request, under its own privacy policy. The app sends nothing on its own.

Notifications and permissions

The app requests no special permissions — no notifications, location, contacts, camera, or photos.

Children

Job Trail is a general-audience productivity app and does not knowingly collect information from anyone, including children under 13. (It collects nothing from anyone.)

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, we'll update this page and note what changed.
